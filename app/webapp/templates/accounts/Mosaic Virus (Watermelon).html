Mosaic Virus (Watermelon) Light and dark green mottling on leaves, leaf curling, stunted growth, and reduced fruit quality.
  • Remove and destroy infected plants immediately to prevent spread.
  • Use insecticidal soap or neem oil to control aphids, which spread the virus.
  • Introduce beneficial insects like ladybugs to reduce aphid populations.
  • Plant virus-resistant watermelon varieties.
  • Maintain proper spacing between plants to improve airflow.
  • Use reflective mulch to deter insect vectors.
  • Avoid working in the field when plants are wet to reduce virus transmission.
  • Control weeds that may harbor the virus and insect vectors.
  • Apply compost and organic fertilizers to boost plant immunity.
  • Rotate crops with non-host plants to break the virus cycle.
  • Sanitize garden tools regularly to prevent mechanical transmission.
  • Encourage soil biodiversity with organic matter to support healthy plants.
  • Use row covers to protect young plants from insect vectors.
